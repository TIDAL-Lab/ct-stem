Exploring Climate Change

These questions use a computer model that simulates the effect of cloud cover and carbon dioxide (CO2) on the temperature of earth. To run the simulation, click on the Setup button and then click on the Go button to start the model. CO2 is shown as green and white molecules in the atmosphere. To run the simulation again, press the Setup button. A graph above the simulation shows the global temperature over the time. You can change the speed of the model with the slider at the top of the model. If you do not see the model above, please click here.

Try running the model a few times now. There are two sliders that adjust the cloud coverage and carbon dioxide (CO2) in the atmosphere.

In the model, the yellow lines represent sunlight. Some of the sunlight reflects off the earth’s surface while some of the sunlight is absorbed. The red dots represent heat energy that has been absorbed. Some of this heat energy is emitted back towards the atmosphere in the form of infrared (IR) light. The infrared light is show as red lines.

Heat Transfer in Metal Experiment

A materials scientist wants to understand how heat transfers through a metal bar. She attached four temperature sensors evenly along the length of the bar as shown in the picture. She then applied a heat source to one end of the bar.
She wrote this computer program to record temperature readings from the sensors: 
repeat forever:
   value1 = readSensor(1)
   value2 = readSensor(2)
   value3 = readSensor(3)
   value4 = readSensor(4)
   print 1, value1, time
   print 2, value2, time
   print 3, value3, time
   print 4, value4, time

   pause 20 seconds
Item 6: Which of these output fragments was generated by her program?
Item 7: The scientist now wants to make a graph of her data. Which of these graphs would be best to show how all of the sensor readings changed over time?

Item 8: When the scientist ran her trial with four sensors, she noticed something strange with the third sensor. This graph shows the readings for sensors 2 and 3.
Which explanation is most likely?
  1. Sensor 3 is working correctly. There is always noise in sensor readings.
  2. The heat source got disconnected.
  3. Sensor 3 broke after the trial finished.
  4. Sensor 3 was accidentally disconnected during the trial.
The scientist now wants to measure temperature change at 12 points evenly spaced along the bar. The problem is that she only has 4 sensors that she can use.

  3. Run several trials and move the sensors to different locations between the trials.
